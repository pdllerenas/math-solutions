Solutions to some of the exercises in Sheldon Axler's Linear Algebra Done Right (4th edition).

Chapter 2

1. Prove that $-(-v) = v$ for all $v \in V$.

Proof. From 1.32 we know that $-v = (-1)v$, so \[ \begin{align*} -(-v) &= (-1)[(-1)v] \\ &= [(-1)(-1)]v \\ &= 1v\\ &= v. \end{align*} \]   $\square$

2. Suppose $a\in \mathbb{F}$, $v \in V$, and $av = 0$. Prove that $a = 0$ or $v = 0$.

Proof. Assume $a \neq 0$ and $v \neq 0$. Then $1/a \in \mathbb{F}$, so that \[ \begin{align*} v &= 1v \\ &= (1/a \cdot a )v \\ &= \frac{1}{a}(av) \\ &= \frac{1}{a}(0) \\ &= 0. \end{align*} \] Which is a contradiction. Thus, $a = 0$ or $v = 0$.   $\square$
