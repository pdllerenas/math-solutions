1. For each of parts (a) through (d), indicate whether we would generally expect the performance of a fexible statistical learning method to be better or worse than an infexible method. Justify your answer.

  • The sample size n is extremely large, and the number of predictors p is small.
  • The number of predictors p is extremely large, and the number of observations n is small.
  • The relationship between the predictors and response is highly non-linear.
  • The variance of the error terms, i.e. $\sigma^2 = Var(\epsilon)$, is extremely high.
Solution.
  • Given that flexible methods generally have a higher variance than inflexible methods, we would expect flexible methods to perform poorly when the sample sizes are larger. Moreover, by the law of large numbers, having a larger n would allow the inflexible method to more reliably predict the response.
  • Having a larger amount of predictors makes it more difficult to model the relationship between the predictors and response. Moreover, having a small sample size causes flexible methods to overfit very easily, but inflexible methods underfit. Both methods are most likely perfoming equally.
  • A flexible approach would perform better, as it would adhere to the non-linearity of the relationship between the predictors and response.
  • An inflexible approach would perform better, as the amount of variance would not affect it as much.
 

2. Explain whether each scenario is a classifcation or regression problem, and indicate whether we are most interested in inference or prediction. Finally, provide n and p.

  • We collect a set of data on the top 500 firms in the US. For each firm we record proft, number of employees, industry and the CEO salary. We are interested in understanding which factors afect CEO salary.
  • We are considering launching a new product and wish to know whether it will be a success or a failure. We collect data on 20 similar products that were previously launched. For each product we have recorded whether it was a success or failure, price charged for the product, marketing budget, competition price, and ten other variables.
  • We are interested in predicting the % change in the USD/Euro exchange rate in relation to the weekly changes in the world stock markets. Hence we collect weekly data for all of 2012. For each week we record the % change in the USD/Euro, the % change in the US market, the % change in the British market, and the % change in the German market.
Solution.
  • We should use regression, as we are interested in viewing the relation between the variables given. Moreover, this is an inference problem, as we simply wish to understand the impact of each variable on previous records. Finally, we have $n = 500$ and $p = 3$.
  • This is a classification problem, as to whether a product will succeed or not. Moreover, we are more interested in prediction, as we need to know if, given certain parameters, the product will succeed. Finally, we have $n = 20$ and $p = 13$.
  • This is a regression problem aiming to predict the % change in the mentioned exchange rate. Finally, we have $n = 4 * 12 = 48$ (weeks) and $p = 3$.
